3. Trial Period

All new installations begin with a 30-day trial period. During the trial, all functionality is available without a license key. The trial period begins on the date of first use.

At the end of the trial period, your Atested deployment generates an attestation artifact showing your usage. This artifact recommends your appropriate tier based on the number of unique users observed during the trial.

If your trial usage qualifies for the Personal tier (single user, non-commercial), you may continue using Atested at no cost. If your usage qualifies for a paid tier, you should purchase a license key within 14 days of trial expiration.

4. Renewal and Tier Adjustment

License keys are valid for one year from the date of purchase. At renewal, your Atested deployment generates a current attestation artifact. Your renewal tier is determined by this attestation:

  • If your unique user count has increased beyond your current tier, your renewal tier adjusts upward.
  • If your unique user count has decreased below your current tier threshold, your renewal tier adjusts downward.
  • Tier adjustments are effective at renewal, not mid-term.

5. The Evidentiary Enforcement Model

Atested uses an evidentiary enforcement model. This means:

  • Atested does not lock out unlicensed users. It does not implement DRM, activation checks, or runtime restrictions that prevent the software from functioning.
  • The governance layer records the license status in every decision record. If you are unlicensed, your governance records reflect that fact.
  • The system records what is true. An unlicensed deployment produces governance records that show the license status as unlicensed. A licensed deployment produces records showing the license status as licensed.
  • Your attestation artifacts — the same artifacts you would show an auditor or customer — reflect your actual license status.
